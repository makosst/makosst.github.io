# About Me

Born in Odesa, Ukraine. 

**8 y.o.:** 
Started learning how to code, did some Arduino. 

**12 y.o.:**
Was actively learning C#, completed a course with a bunch of 25+ folks who we learning alongside me. It was kind of a niche group, and we ended up trying to do a startup and even worked in some office for some time after the course. 

**14 y.o.:**
Found a job by posting my Unity game on Reddit. Worked there remotely for half a year after school. Was doing hypercasual games and figured that I probably can do them myself without the manager. Saved a few $k. 

**15 y.o.:** 
Started my gaming company, tried to cofound with other school students, we had a slack workspace even, though no one really wanted to spend time on this. Ended up doing it solo and hiring one of the guys as an engineer. 

Reached out to publishers, went to conferences and set up meetings there, as a result we had some publishers that we started working with for free, and later on landed agreements that allowed us to actually earn money. 

**17 y.o.:** 
Around $10K monthly revenue, $6K net. The team scaled to 6 employees in Ukraine, several contracts with publishers who paid us for each of the games we built, and a pipeline that was creating them pretty quickly. 

**18 y.o.:**
As Apple introduced ATT, gaming publishers earned less revenue, and less studios were getting any money from them. So our renewals were not happening and contracts were slowly getting cancelled. 

I tried to pivot into building a bigger game - we built a prototype, tried to raise funding on it, and failed. At the end I was left with nothing and the company itself closed.

**20 y.o.:**
Moved to SF, spent a lot of time trying to build something people want, some projects got very slight traction (a few startups in founders inc. subscribed to use my rendering service), graph-based coding agent I built was able to dissasemble large repos into interactive graphs, and this was added to a few of the big repositories, but did not get massive real usage. 

**21 y.o.:**
Got on a first job in a while, as engineer #2 at Echelon. Built AI pipelines that scaled us from first pilots to being adopted by a few mid-scale enterprises and PE firms. This allowed the team to scale and get on a pretty steep growth trajectory.
